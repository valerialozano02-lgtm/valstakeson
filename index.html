Val's Take on Legalities

May 25, 2026

After watching the first 10 minutes of Margot's Got Money Troubles, I thought to myself: mmh, maybe I could have a baby to stay in the country. As in, the United States of America. But then I watched her give birth with no epidural and nurse the baby, and the thought of a small human latching onto my nipple while sucking me for liquid nourishment did not sound appealing. Bottom line: having a baby in the United States would not make me a citizen. Period.

As you readers are wondering why I want to become a US citizen, well, I think it would strengthen my chances of landing a good job. I graduated college three weeks ago, and I feel incompetent for not doing anything all day. I wake up, go on LinkedIn, hit apply, and wait for an email. I lie about having authorization to work in the country, hoping my resume will pass the shitty ATS system. I also thought about opening an OnlyFans account; I even googled the steps for it and how fast I could make money off of it.

Another important fact to note: I have the kind of privilege where money isn't the problem. I don't have any student loans, nor do my parents have any loans after paying for my college degree. I have a Bachelor of Science in Integrated Marketing Communications. I learned everything there is to learn in college, academically speaking, that is. I even learned how to code and calculate revenue for a dispensable product that would probably lose more money than the actual investment it took to make, but I got very good at telling a client that it would actually sell and make more money than what it took to make. It's not lying, it's merely not making people worry. Think of it as a survival instinct.

Anyway, back to business. So here I am, a recent grad with no prospects whatsoever, no job opportunities, and no proposals regarding my love life. Because yes, before you say anything, I've thought about marrying some American guy just for the citizenship. But I've realized my type of man is the one who never says no to me. But that's a story for another time.

So here I am, waiting for nice-looking Arab man to send me my resume so I can get a decent job.

I must be boring you guys with all my unemployment talk, but not much has been going on in my life besides watching Love Island, which I will be writing about, so just you wait. Also, is it cliché that I'm doing this? I really don't care. Maybe something good will come out of this, but in the meantime, I'm looking for a job, if any of my readers have ideas or suggestions on how to survive life post-grad, please do tell, I'm all ears.

Val's Take on Boredom

June 30, 2026

I started writing when I was a kid. I had a sparkly diary from Justice with my initials on it, full of sparkles and covered in leopard fabric. You could have easily mistaken it for a pimp's agenda. What I used to write about was nothing special, mostly poetry about how my heart ached that Shawn Mendes was never going to be mine, or some cheesy romance book I should not have been reading at the ripe age of twelve years old.

I did grow up with no digital supervision whatsoever. Blame my parents, not me, although if you ask me, I turned out just fine. I did not hurry my childhood, adolescence, or adulthood. I was right where I was meant to be, and still am. My dad did, however, introduce me to the grotesque masterpiece that is The Godfather at a very young age. The first time I saw Al Pacino on screen, my heart sped up, and I can proudly say my first crush was Michael Corleone. Sylvester Stallone, let's not leave Rocky out of this either.

You might have also noticed that I get sidetracked a lot. Back to business.

So I knew that I could write, but what was I to do with this gift? Well, share it with the world, or try to. I would write everything down in notebooks. From ages 14 to 23, I had my life documented in pen and paper, and now online (this is my digital footprint). But somewhere along the way, I got this fixation that if my life was not interesting, or if nothing exciting was going on, the pages did not deserve my pen. So I stopped writing. (That is a story for another day, you will get it, don't worry.)

This went on for at least six months, maybe even a year. But up until recently, something happened. By "something" I mean one of my mother's pep talks that actually sounds like she is mad at me. And I realized that my life is not defined by my achievements, my travels, or the people I meet along the way, but rather by the way I choose to react to what happens to me and be content with the boring. Because in all honesty, life is boring. It is me who decides to make it interesting.

And to some of my readers, this realization may have come a long time ago. But I tend to take a while to analyze patterns in my life, maybe because I am in denial, or simply because I am stubborn and decide to do things all by myself.

Bottom line: my life will always be worthy of documentation, and I think yours should be too.

Val's Take on Nightmares

July 7, 2026

Sorry to disappoint, but this is not the review of Love Island Season 8 and all my humble thoughts and opinions on it. Today I am writing about an epiphany I had.

If anyone knows me personally, they would describe my personality as intense. I beg to differ. I'd say determined. I believe things happen for a reason, and if I don't get something, I will do everything in my absolute power to get it. No matter the lengths I have to go to. When I want something, I will get it.

How does that usually work, you might ask? Well, I start by formulating a plan, write that shit down, pros and cons it, and then do it. I usually say a prayer or two, or beg and beg the Man Upstairs to please let me have it. But I've realized that never lasts. I have the thing I want for a few months, and then that thing goes away. One time it worked. I lost something, then nine months passed, and I got it back. But the thing only lasted nine months.

So in this season of waiting, I've realized that thinking about the "worst case scenario" might actually be the best case scenario. It's not giving up when you look for alternatives to how to solve a situation besides the original idea you had in your head. In the end, only 8% of our plans tend to work out (yes, this statistic is true, look it up).

So what am I getting at here, Val? Well, readers, what I'm getting at is this: what if the thing you really wanted, with heart, body, and soul, ends up hurting you? I don't mean give up and find something else. No. But let it play out, and see if it works out. And if it doesn't, maybe it's because of what would have happened if it did. What version of yourself would come out of it? A good one? Or one you always vowed to never be?

This may or may not apply to you. And if you believe in God, or another higher being that sails your boat, let it be. Put your everything into everything you do, but don't let it ruin you, consume you, become the only thing you can possibly think about. What if you are being protected? From what, I don't know. But the world works in unquestionable ways.

Thinking about the alternative is good, and most of the time we don't like the alternative, but this is good. You are exposing your brain to fear, and when the brain is exposed to fear, it becomes braver. And yes, look it up, this is called neuroplasticity: the brain's ability to rewrite pathways. Rather than permanently erasing fear, the brain learns to regulate it.

(Wow, Val, you are so smart, and you did your research.) Yes, I did. It's not at all bullshit that comes out of my overthinking. I just like statistics and facts, they help me calm down.

So readers, don't be afraid to think about your worst nightmare.

All nightmares end.
